ASTHMA ATTACK: HOW TO HELP

During an asthma attack, it's crucial to stay calm and assist the person in finding relief. Encourage them to use their prescribed rescue inhaler, which contains fast-acting medications like albuterol. If the person is unable to speak or if their breathing worsens, seek emergency medical help immediately. You can learn more about the steps to take during an asthma emergency by visiting The Asthma and Allergy Foundation of America for guidance.

In addition to using their inhaler, individuals may benefit from sitting up straight and breathing slowly. Help them stay calm, as anxiety can exacerbate their symptoms. It's essential to monitor their condition closely and be prepared to call emergency services if necessary. For more tips on managing asthma attacks, check out Mayo Clinic.
